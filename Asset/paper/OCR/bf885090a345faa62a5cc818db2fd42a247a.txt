3.7. ScoreGAN
In their study, Shehnepoor et al. [95] proposed ScoreGAN to detect online shopping
fraud. This novel framework generates reviews with precise semantics to address fraud
detections’ lack of high-quality data. ScoreGAN uses both review ratings and review text
scores to detect and generate progression. The findings of this study show that customi-
zation of the machine-obtained reviews based on the score leads to considerable progress
in detecting fraud reviews by 5% on Tripadvisor and by 7% on the Yelp dataset against
the data obtained from the state-of-the-art systems.
3.7.1. Process
1. The Discriminator D differentiates between human fraud reviews from fraud bot
reviews, and calculates the probability of a score based on fraud reviews and cor-
responding scores.
2. After that, the Discriminator can differentiate genuine reviews from fraud reviews.
3. On the other hand, the Generator takes the score and random noise, generating fake
bot reviews.
3.7.2. Strengths
1. This framework can convert the discrete form into a continuous one. This research
work used the Tripadvisor and Yelp datasets, which are more reliable than datasets
labeled by humans.
2. This proposed method outperforms other systems when applied to the used dataset,
according to the metrics.
